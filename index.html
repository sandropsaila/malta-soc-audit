Audit Frameworks

SOC 2 (ISAE 3000)
Evaluates security, availability, processing integrity, confidentiality, and privacy controls. Type I assesses design; Type II assesses operating effectiveness over 6–12 months. International equivalent of ISAE 3000.
SOC 1
Assesses internal controls relevant to clients' financial reporting. Governed by SSAE 18 (US) or ISAE 3402 (international). Issued as Type I (point-in-time) or Type II (period-of-time).
ISAE 3402
The international equivalent of SOC 1. Provides assurance on service-organisation controls relevant to user entities' financial reporting. Widely used in EU financial services, payroll, and fund administration.
ISAE 3000
The overarching international assurance standard for non-financial engagements, covering information security, GDPR, ESG, and more. SOC 2 reports issued internationally typically comply with ISAE 3000.

MFSA-Regulated: Payment & Trading Platforms

⚖️ Regulatory Requirement — MFSA Investment Services Rules

Under Rule R1-1.12 (System Certification) of Part BI of the MFSA Investment Services Rules for MiFID Firms, and Rule R13-13.6 (Supplementary Rules for CFD/Rolling Spot Forex Trading Organisations), licence holders operating electronic trading systems must ensure those systems undergo independent assessment by a qualified third party. Acceptable formats include ISAE 3000, ISAE 3402, SOC 2, PCI DSS (for payment flows), ISO 27001 surveillance audits, and bespoke regulatory assurance engagements commissioned under the MFSA Guidance on Technology Arrangements & ICT. For payment institutions licensed under the Financial Institutions Act (FIR/02, FIR/03), equivalent independent IT audit obligations apply. The rule broadly mirrors Rule 14.6 of Part A (Binary Options / Trading Organisations) which requires systems to be independently tested and certified prior to live operation and following material updates.

Source: MFSA Part BI Rules (December 2024) · Part A: The Application Process (October 2025)

Data Sources & Methodology

Company data was cross-referenced against the Tech.mt Members Directory (556 companies), the MGA Licensee Register (575 licensees), and the MFSA Investment Services Licence Holders register. MFSA regulatory context is drawn from the Investment Services Rules Part BI (December 2024) and Part A (October 2025), specifically Sections 12, 13, and 14 covering System Certification, CFD/Forex Trading Organisations, and Binary Options providers. Companies listed as "Monitoring" or "System Cert Required" have obligations but have not publicly confirmed the format of their independent assessments. PCI DSS Level 1 QSA audits are treated as equivalent to ISAE 3402 for payment processing control assurance.
