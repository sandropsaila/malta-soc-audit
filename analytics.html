Key Findings

57%
SOC 2 Dominance
8 of 14 companies hold or are working toward SOC 2, making it the most prevalent framework across Malta's regulated sectors — driven by crypto exchange MiCA requirements and iGaming B2B supplier due diligence.
5
Crypto / MiCA Entities
Crypto & trading is the fastest-growing sector — Gemini, Crypto.com, Bitstamp and Nuvei all hold MFSA MiCA or MiFID licences that directly trigger DORA and system certification obligations.
3
ISAE 3402 Holders
ISAE 3402 remains the standard of choice for iGaming operators (Greentube) and IT infrastructure providers (Hornetsecurity) that need to demonstrate financial control assurance to B2B clients and fund administrators.
4
Obligation — No Public Report
4 MFSA-licensed firms (Deriv, eToro, Kambi, Arrise) have clear regulatory obligations under R13-13.6 or MGA B2B rules but have not publicly disclosed independent assurance reports — a notable gap.
100%
Deloitte in Crypto
Every confirmed SOC audit in the crypto/exchange sector was conducted by Deloitte — reflecting the Big-4 firm's dominant position in crypto-native assurance for AICPA and IAASB standards globally.
2025
DORA Inflection Point
The EU's Digital Operational Resilience Act (DORA) — in force January 2025 — is expected to drive a wave of new ISAE 3000 and SOC 2 engagements from MFSA-licensed payment institutions and investment firms in 2026.

Notes on Methodology

Analysis is based on 14 companies identified across the Tech.mt Members Directory, MGA Licensee Register, and MFSA Investment Services register. Companies holding multiple certifications are counted in each applicable framework category. "Monitoring" companies (no public report confirmed) are excluded from confirmed counts but included in sector totals. Timeline dates reflect the year of first publicly confirmed or announced engagement. Data correct as of May 2026.
