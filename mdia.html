The MDIA & SOC / ISAE Reporting in Malta

How Malta's Digital Innovation Authority (MDIA), its Technology Assurance Sandbox (MDIA-TAS), and the Innovative Technology Arrangement (ITA) certification regime intersect with international SOC and ISAE assurance frameworks — and why ISAE 3000 sits at the heart of it.

What is the MDIA?

The Malta Digital Innovation Authority (MDIA) was established under the Malta Digital Innovation Authority Act, Chapter 591 of the Laws of Malta (2018) as the country's regulator for innovative technologies. It was originally created to regulate Distributed Ledger Technology (DLT) and smart contracts under Malta's pioneering "Blockchain Island" framework, and its mandate was significantly expanded in 2024 by Act No. XIX of 2024 to cover Artificial Intelligence and other innovative technologies.

The MDIA's regulatory tools work alongside the Innovative Technology Arrangements and Services Act (ITAS Act) (Cap. 592), which gives the Authority power to certify Innovative Technology Arrangements (ITAs) — including DLT systems, smart contracts, decentralised oracles, and AI systems — as functionally correct, secure, and aligned with internationally recognised control objectives.

Crucially, the MDIA is also Malta's designated cybersecurity certification authority and the competent authority for the EU Data Governance Act, putting it at the centre of Malta's cross-cutting digital trust framework alongside the MFSA (financial services) and MGA (gaming).

Core Functions

  • Certification of ITAs — voluntary registration and certification of DLT systems, smart contracts, and AI solutions against published Control Objectives
  • Registration of Technology Service Providers — under the ITAS Act
  • MDIA Technology Assurance Sandbox (MDIA-TAS) — an iterative two-year residency for start-ups and SMEs
  • Accreditation of Systems Auditors — independent third parties licensed to perform conformity assessments under ISAE 3000
  • National AI Strategy — co-ordinating Malta's implementation of the EU AI Act (effective June 2024)
  • Cybersecurity certification authority under the EU Cybersecurity Act
⚖️ Key Connection
MDIA System Audits Are Conducted Under ISAE 3000

Per the official MDIA accreditation framework, "the audit conducted by the Systems Auditor must follow the ISAE 3000 standard" — the international standard for assurance over non-financial information. This makes ISAE 3000 the de facto link between Malta's national technology assurance regime and the international SOC 2 ecosystem, since SOC 2 reports issued outside the United States are typically prepared in accordance with ISAE 3000 themselves.

In practice, an MDIA-certified ITA provides assurance over functional correctness of the technology arrangement (does the smart contract actually do what its blueprint says?), while a SOC 2 report provides assurance over the operating environment (security, availability, confidentiality of the systems running it). For DLT and AI service providers in Malta, the two complement each other rather than substitute for each other.

Malta's "Blockchain Island" framework — Cap. 591 (MDIA) and Cap. 592 (ITAS) — comes into force on 1 November 2018, alongside the Virtual Financial Assets Act. System Audits become mandatory for VFA Act licence applicants.

MDIA accredits the first round of Systems Auditors — PwC Malta, KPMG Malta, BDO Technology Advisory Limited and STIS Group Ltd. Malta becomes the first jurisdiction with a national, cryptographically-verifiable certification scheme for innovative technology.

Malta becomes "the world's first national AI certification programme" via the AI Innovative Technology Arrangement (AI ITA) scheme — pre-dating the EU AI Act by more than four years.
June 2022

The Technology Assurance Sandbox is upgraded to v2.0, designed for start-ups and small companies. Up to two-year residency, phased Control Objective alignment, and access to government grants substantially covering Systems Auditor assessment fees.
November 2022

MDIA certifies Fact Technologies Ltd as its 5th Systems Auditor. Each certificate is now cryptographically verifiable via SHA-512 and timestamped on the public Bitcoin blockchain.
June 2024

The EU AI Act becomes directly applicable in Malta. MDIA is designated lead authority for AI Act implementation.
2024
Act XIX of 2024 — expanded MDIA powers
The Malta Digital Innovation Authority (Amendment) Act expands the MDIA's mandate beyond DLT to all innovative technology — explicitly including AI — and removes earlier restrictions.
2024 / 2025

The ITAS Certification Regulations (S.L. 591.01) are updated to reflect the broader scope, the new MDIA-TAS framework, and harmonisation with EU AI, DORA, NIS 2, and Cyber Resilience Act obligations.

Technology Assurance Sandbox (MDIA-TAS)

The MDIA-TAS is the Authority's flagship utility for innovative technology developers — a regulatory sandbox specifically designed to give start-ups and smaller companies a structured, affordable pathway to MDIA certification. It addresses one of the longest-standing criticisms of the original 2018 ITA framework: that full certification was prohibitively expensive for small teams.

How It Works

  1. Pre-engagement. The applicant engages with MDIA to confirm Malta substance — either the development is carried out in Malta, or the operations are carried out in or from Malta.
  2. Residency (up to 2 years). The applicant enters the sandbox and selects which Control Objectives apply at which phase, allowing flexible alignment with the team's development plan.
  3. Phased assessments. At the end of each phase, an independent third-party technical assessment is conducted by an MDIA-certified Systems Auditor, following ISAE 3000.
  4. Iteration. The applicant addresses any findings; the Auditor re-tests; the cycle continues until all Control Objectives are met.
  5. Full MDIA certification. Issued once all Control Objectives are satisfied.

Why It Matters

  • Low barrier to entry. Administrative cost is just €500, and the Systems Auditor assessment fees can be covered by Government grants.
  • Designed for SMEs. Especially start-ups in DLT, AI, and critical-systems development that cannot absorb Big-4 audit fees up-front.
  • Cryptographic verification. Each certificate has a SHA-512 signature and is timestamped on the Bitcoin blockchain — meaning anyone can verify the authenticity of an MDIA certificate independently.
  • EU regulatory alignment. The TAS is designed as a single venue where applicants can also align with DORA, NIS 2, the Cyber Resilience Act, and the EU AI Act.
"By creating the Technology Assurance Sandbox, the MDIA envisaged an innovative concept which provides a safe environment for individuals and companies developing solutions on Innovative Technologies, such as Blockchain, AI and critical systems, to enable the correct nurturing of such solutions in line with recognised standards." — MDIA-TAS programme description, via tech.mt

The Five MDIA-Certified Systems Auditors

Only five firms in Malta currently hold MDIA Systems Auditor certification, making them the exclusive pool of independent third parties able to issue ISAE 3000 conformity assessments for ITAs. The Layered Reality for a Malta DLT or AI Service Provider

A VFA-licensed crypto custodian operating from Malta typically holds three different reports addressing three different audiences:

  • A SOC 2 Type II demonstrates to enterprise customers and counterparties that its security, availability, and confidentiality controls operated effectively over the past year.
  • A SOC 1 / ISAE 3402 demonstrates to its clients' financial auditors that the controls relevant to those clients' financial statements (custody balances, transaction processing) operated effectively.
  • An MDIA ITA Certification (where applicable) demonstrates to the MDIA, regulators, and the Maltese public that the technology itself — the smart contract, the DLT system, or the AI model — does what its published blueprint says it does.

Because all three are anchored in ISAE 3000 / 3402 international standards, the underlying control catalogues, audit methodology, and evidence requirements significantly overlap. This is why Malta's leading audit firms (PwC, KPMG, BDO, RSM, EY, Grant Thornton) typically run integrated technology-assurance practices that can issue all three with shared evidence collection.

Which Sectors Use MDIA & SOC/ISAE Reporting in Malta?

Mandatory Use Cases

  • VFA Act licensees — Issuers and Service Providers applying to the MFSA under the Virtual Financial Assets Act must obtain a Type 1 System Audit before licence award. Type 2 audits are conducted periodically thereafter.
  • MDIA Sandbox tenants — Mandatory Systems Auditor assessments at each phase.
  • MGA Sandbox tenants — A similar assurance process is obligatory for Malta Gaming Authority Sandbox participants using DLT.

Voluntary But Strategically Important

  • iGaming B2B suppliers — Major B2C operators increasingly require ISAE 3402 / SOC 2 from their critical suppliers as part of the MGA's B2B Critical Supply framework. Greentube Malta's ISAE 3402 (early 2023) is a leading example.
  • MFSA-licensed payment institutions and EMIs — Subject to FIR/02 and FIR/03 IT audit obligations equivalent to ISAE 3000 / SOC 2.
  • MFSA Investment Services licensees (MiFID firms) — Subject to Rule R1-1.12 (System Certification) of Part BI and Rule R13-13.6 for CFD/Forex trading organisations.
  • Cloud and IT infrastructure providers serving Malta (e.g. BMIT, Hornetsecurity) — SOC 2 / ISAE 3402 used as differentiation in EU enterprise sales.
  • AI service providers — As Malta's AI ITA scheme matures alongside the EU AI Act, voluntary MDIA certification of AI systems is expected to become a recognised compliance signal under high-risk AI categories.
📌 Bottom Line
Malta's Distinctive Position

Malta is one of very few EU jurisdictions where a single national authority co-ordinates a regulatory technology-assurance regime that explicitly anchors itself in ISAE 3000. This has two consequences:

(1) For Malta-based service providers, the MDIA framework provides a unique, internationally credible signal of technology trustworthiness — particularly valuable for DLT, smart-contract, and AI deployments where conventional SOC 2 reports cannot speak to functional correctness.

(2) For international service providers entering Malta (including MiCA CASPs choosing Malta as their EU hub), the MDIA / MFSA / MGA assurance ecosystem creates a layered compliance landscape where SOC 2, ISAE 3402, MDIA ITA, and PCI DSS reports often need to coexist. The five MDIA-certified Systems Auditors — PwC, KPMG, BDO, STIS, and Fact Technologies — are positioned as the natural single-point providers across these adjacent regimes.
